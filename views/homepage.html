FINAL PROJECT

Bouquet of Currency

For my final project I created a series of responsive "flowers" of currency which open and close relative to the strength of that currency.

Initial Brainstorming

At the start of this project, I wasn't quite sure what I wanted to do. I knew I wanted to design a sculpture that responded to something current which changed rapidly. To start off I wanted to think a little about what kinds of things were rapidly changing in this environment as well as what pieces of news I would be most interestested in reflecting in the artwork. While there are many possible pieces I'd like to include I settled on three things to start -- money, food, and medical supplies. I decided to start with money. The stock market is EXTREAMLY volitile right now, which I think makes it a great subject for this type of responsive artwork. Thus, the first piece I've decided to create is a piece that responds to daily information about money. (I could of course make this hourly as well but I deicded generally to work with the idea of the sculpture changing once at noon every day -- I can adjust this later throughout the project if necessary). It is also important to note that I was VERY determined to mkae this project soley out of household items (with a little bit of arduino :) )

Initial Design

After drawing up a few different designs I decided one I liked is an accordian crumpling of a bill. I could later exapnd this to mutilple billls and sort of use the response as a bar graph (with the strech size of the bill being the maximum size on the bar.) For now I am working with a $1 bill in case I break it... but I'd like to use a fake $100 dollar bill if its possible to get one of these days.

Design Iteration

Although I liked this "accordian-type" bill, it wasn't quite that pretty. I ultimately wanted to create an art piece so I instead started to think of a different way of having the money contract and expand... what if instead it was like a flower? After playing around with many different designs and iterations -- and using absolutely whatever I could find around the house... I came up with the following design. Below I have described in detail how to create my money flowers.

Step-By-Step: How to Create a Money Flower

Materials for a Money Flower
  • A large circular key ring
  • 4 safety pins with holes large enough to allow them to slide onto the key ring
  • 4 bobby pins
  • Wire or string the size of the hole at the top of the safety pins (see below for detail)
  • A large paperclip.
  • Lots of hot glue, tap or some other fasteners.
  • A willingness to replace any of the above items with whatever you can find in your house.
Step 1:

Bend each of the bobby pins to a right angle. Use clippers, scissor a saw or anything that cuts to trim the wavy part of the bobby pins at the first wave. Repeat for all four bobby pins.

Step 2:

Open and bend each of the safety pins to extend just beyond a right angle.

Step 3:

Place down a layer of clear duct tape (sticky side up!) And place the long part of the bobby pins along the tape with the short end pointing upwards. (note extra bobby pins are pictured here as I was making more than one flower.)

Step 4:

Place the pointed end of the saftey pin along the bobby pin. Press against the sticky tape to ensure it remains upright. Then, use hot glue at the connection site to ensure a secure bond.

Step 5:

Allow the glue to completely dry and set, lift the bobbypin-safetypin joint off the tape and put more hot glue on the other side of the connection. Again allow to dry and set completely.

Step 6:

Take the metal keyring and bend the secondary half dowards, ensure that there is still one fully connected circle after your adjustments.

Step 7:

Slide the bobby pins onto the key ring, with the safety pin head facing inwards and the bobby pin pointing head, pointing away from the stretched part of the key ring.

Step 8:

Thread a piece of thick string or thin wire through the small hot in the head of the four safety pins, and hot glue the ends of it together to form a circle.

Step 9:

Take a paper clip and completely unbend it. Wrap one end of the clip-wire tightgy around the string/wire connecting the heads of the four safetypins.

You should now be able to push the paper clip up and down to "open and close" the bobby pins. The mechanics of your flower are complete!

Assembly of My Money Bouquet

To assemble the money bouquet, I twisted the bottom of the paperclip wire, and looped it through one of the holes on the white spinning blade of a servo motor. I then taped the flower onto the side of a vase and tested the motor. Only after testing the mechanics I attached the money! I repeated this for each of the flowers I wanted to use.
