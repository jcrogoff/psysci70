After some additional research I found this tutorial online about how to make a shadow light box. I didn't follow this tutorial exaxtly, rather used the steps he did for inspiration for my process and project.

Source: https://www.instructables.com/id/LED-Shadow-Box/

I decided I wanted to make a shadow light box with some kind of heart design. I redrew and adapted a heart image I found online to serve my purposes.

I then used the laser cutter to cut out this heart design as well as a backing heart of equal size to be the background for the shadow box.

I then decided instead of just making an ON-OFF light box I wanted to make one which was sensitive to light -- that way my lightbox could detect when the lights were off and provide ~mood lighting~ when needed. So now its time to move to coding (thank goodness). To do this light detection I used a phototransistor. First, I just wanted to get the phototransitor to work and see how it detected light. I found the following circuit off a paper on pubmed. I used this circuit and then wrote my own program which would just report the reading of the phototransitor to the computer console.
