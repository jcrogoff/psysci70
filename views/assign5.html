3D DESIGN

Our assignment this week was to design and 3D print something not easily made with subtractive methods. I started off doing some research on what 3D printers are used for in professional product construction. I thought this would be a good place to look as people are unlikely to use 3D printers in professional circumstances if they are not sinficantly helpful (as printers are quite expensive). I realized that for the most part, the argument was to use 3D printers to make parts that would otherwise need lots of asssembly. I found images of these lattice structures online -- which are used as a way to have strong but less densse material. These are very difficult to make by subtractice methods

Source: https://www.quora.com/What-things-can-be-manufactured-with-a-3D-printer-that-are-impossible-to-make-otherwise

So I set out to make one of these structures. However, it became clear very quickly that my skills in fusion were greatly limiting my ability to build such a complex structure. So then I started thinking about what is kind of difficult to assemble precisely. I realized things with curves are extreamly difficult. I then started to play around in fusion and came up with the follow design which has curved edges, a sphere trapped on the inside, and a sloping top.

I wish I had taken some more images as I was playing around because its difficult to see the sphere insisde the object. If there's one thing I learned from this design expirence in fusion it is that next time I really need to take the time to make more bodies. Right now it is virtually impossible to edit the size of the sphere, or to put anything else inside the object, because it is all one component. Next time I will separate everything into multiple components that way I can edit one thing at a time without having to start the whole component from scratch. This division would also have made it easier to take screenshots of the different angles of my object.

SLICING

Next, I fed my 3D design into the slicer. Because my design was quite large and complex, I chose the print setting 0.20mm SPEED MK3. Which was one step of priciseness above the draft stage but still quite fast MK3. I also set the infill at 10% as Rob said this was sufficient and would still print quickly.
