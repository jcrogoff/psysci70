Our assignment this week was to create something that moves (with joints -- although I got special permission to sort of ignore that requirement). I was really interested in perhaps modeling some of the sculptures of Joshua Allen Harris. An artist who was inspired by all the trash on New York city streets. Joshua Allen Harris used trash bags and wind from the New York subway to create whimsical art pieces.

I decided I wanted to try to make a camel as that is my favorite animal. I started off by doing some modeling with paper as this was faster than working with the trash bags and gave me some idea about scale.

I then moved to the trash bags. This material ended up being a lot more difficult to work with than I anticipated. It actually took a surprising number of hours to arrive at the camel you see today. Here is a picture of me working as Peter chose to send home the wrong girl last night.

Once I finished I had a LOT of trouble either finding a powerful enough fan or adjusting the cut of the legs and the attachment to the fan to actually get the camel to inflate. This video below shows where I'm at right now -- the camel partially inflates but this fan is pretty weak. I tried a couple different leg hole configurations to try to help it inflate and I found that a diagonal cut was best.

This is the current state of my trash bag camel.

UPDATE -- WE REALIZED THE FAN NEEDED TO BE OFF THE FLOOR TO BLOW UP THE CAMEL!!! IT WORKS!!!
